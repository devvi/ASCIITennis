# Issue 108 — 加入观众席 (Add Audience/ Spectators)

## Motivation

Add visual life and atmosphere to the court by populating the surrounding area with ASCII spectators. The audience reacts emotionally to match events — cheering when the player or opponent performs well — creating a more immersive and rewarding game experience.

## Features

- ASCII-art audience figures seated/standing around the court perimeter
- Spectators placed behind both baselines and along the sidelines (outside court bounds)
- Audience reacts visually (different character / animation) when player or opponent scores a point or hits a good shot
- Cheering animation: spectators raise arms, show excited characters (e.g., `O` → `\o/`, `o/`, etc.)
- Idle animation: subtle swaying or static seated figures when nothing notable happens
- Performance: audience rendering should not degrade frame rate; use simple per-frame updates

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Spectators are rendered around the court in designated seating areas (behind baselines, along sidelines)
- [ ] Each spectator is a small ASCII figure (head + body, e.g. `O` + `|` or `o` + `_`)
- [ ] Spectators face the court (towards center)
- [ ] All spectators together form a recognizable "crowd" in the perspective view
- [ ] Crowd cheers (visually: change character to excited pose) when player scores a point
- [ ] Crowd cheers when AI scores a point or hits a particularly good shot (long rally ends)
- [ ] Crowd returns to idle/gentle sway after cheering subsides
- [ ] Crowd does not overlap with court lines, players, ball, net, or referee
- [ ] Audience density is configurable or set to a reasonable default (e.g. ~20-30 spectators)
- [ ] No new external dependencies — pure ASCII on canvas

## Out of Scope

- Sound effects or audio applause
- Dynamic crowd movement toward the court
- Individual spectator personalities (all spectators react as a group)
- Interactive spectators (cannot be hit by ball)
